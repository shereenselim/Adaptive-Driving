About project

The project aims to address some challenges facing personal vehicles by designing and implementing advanced driving systems to enhance road safety and driving comfort. This includes Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) like Adaptive Cruise Control System, which automatically adjusts the vehicle's speed based on surrounding traffic and can respond in emergency situations. Additionally, the Collision Avoidance System relies on optical sensors and algorithms to prevent accidents and protect passengers. The project also features a Traffic Sign Recognition system that helps drivers to identify road signs and alerts them to important changes like speed limits or hazards, improving safety. The Lane Keep Assist system intervenes when the vehicle drifts out of its lane, guiding it back on course.
Furthermore, the project includes Automatic Parking Assistance System, allowing the car to park safely without driver intervention. Facial recognition System technology is also incorporated, enhancing security by identifying authorized drivers and monitoring their attentiveness. In cases of fainting or sleep, the system takes appropriate actions to ensure driver safety

Key Features of the System:

Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC)

Utilizing technologies such as LIDAR and Raspberry Pi for adaptive speed control, enabling the vehicle to maintain a safe distance from other vehicles on the road.

Collision Avoidance System (CAS)

mploying cameras, proximity sensors, and microcontrollers to detect potential collisions and take corrective actions such as automatic braking.

Facial Recognition and Driver Monitoring

Analyzing driver behavior to detect fatigue or distraction, enhancing overall safety by issuing alerts when necessary.

Lane Keeping Assist (LKA)

Monitoring lane markers using cameras and providing steering corrections to keep the vehicle centered in its lane.

Traffic Sign Recognition (TSR)

Using advanced image processing algorithms to identify and interpret road signs, ensuring the driver is informed of speed limits, stops, and other critical rules.

Automated Parking Assist (APA)

Simplifying parking using ultrasonic sensors and precise control of vehicle movement, including parallel and perpendicular parking scenarios.

Challenges and Solutions:

  • System Integration:

    • Solution: Adopting standardized communication protocols and rigorous testing procedures.
  • User Interface Development:
    • Challenge: Designing a GUI that is both functional and intuitive.
    • Solution: Conducting user research and iterative design processes to meet driver expectations.
  • Real-Time Processing:
    • Challenge: Handling large volumes of sensor data with minimal latency.
    • Solution: Leveraging high-performance computing platforms and optimized algorithms.
  • Scalability:
    • Challenge: Making the system adaptable to different vehicle types and road conditions.
    • Solution: Modular design and flexible software architecture.
